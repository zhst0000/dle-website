On the Record Review

Published: 7/21/2025

Read Length: 5-10 min

On the record is a week-long trivia game where all of the questions are based on quotes from American news, mostly current but also historical. The game is one of six hosted by The Washington Post and represents what is probably their most unique offering.

[CAPTION]

Monday through Thursday the player is offered one quote from the news and must and must answer a question relating to it (e.g. “Who said it?” or “fill in the blank”). The player receives 5 points if they get it on the first try, otherwise they get a hint and can guess again for 3 points before being offered multiple choices where a correct answer yields only 1 point. On Friday the quiz is longer at ten questions instead of only one and it includes a bonus question where the player can choose to wager that day’s points. Your score is summed up throughout the week for a maximum possible score of 120 points. One must log-in every day to attempt that day’s question(s) as there is no way to play previous days.The Friday quiz can be done at any point during the weekend.

[CAPTION]

Question-for-question this game is easily among my favorites in the trivia category. The subject matter is unique, the quotes themselves offer enough context for some educated guessing, and the clue progression makes the game fun even if one doesn't immediately recognise the subject. The game rewards keeping up with current events and having general knowledge in American politics and history. It's a perfect game for those of us who read countless headlines everyday. However, despite all this praise the game does have a glaring flaw.

[CAPTION]

Crucially, this game struggles with being unbalanced in its weekly distribution of questions. It's hardly worth going to find this puzzle for the five seconds of gameplay it provides on most weekdays. Monday through Thursday you'll likely spend more time navigating the post’s website or app to find the game than actually engaging with the content itself. Friday’s edition is better length, certainly on the longer-end of most daily games, but only logging-in on the weekend means missing almost a third of the questions and one sixth of the possible points. Playing only on friday feels somehow empty, while playing everyday feels like a waste of time.

On the record is the winner-by-default of current events trivia games. Every week it offers probably the best trivia experience out there, but to get the full experience you need to do your daily chores.
