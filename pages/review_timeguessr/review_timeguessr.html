TimeGuessr Review

Published: 7/28/2025

Read Length: 3-5 min

Timeguessr is an investigative game about identifing photographs. Scour an image for clues concerning the time and place an image was taken before submitting your final guess. What language is the text in? What era are the cars from? Is the foliage tropical or from somewhere milder? Is the photo even in color? Questions like these narrow down your guess and while creating the satisfying feeling of detective work.

A round of TimeGuessr includes an image, a map to guess the location, and a slider to guess the year.

A game of Timeguessr takes place over five rounds, each with its own picture taken sometime after the year 1900; maybe it was taken during the first World War, maybe it was taken at a concert last week. As previously mentioned, your goal is to identify the time and place the photo was captured using the clues in the image itself. A total of 10,000 points are up for grabs in each round: half for guessing the year and the other half for identifying the location. With more points being awarded for closer guesses.

Where and when was this taken? (Associated Press / Alamy Stock Photo)

The pool of images which this game draws from is its true strength. Photos range from images of well-known historical events from the early nineteenth hundreds to mid century city scapes dotted with classic cars and people in long dated clothes to amateur, cellphone photographs captured as recently as the day before. Some days even include images from recent newsworthy events, meaning that the library of images continues to expand and stay topical. The images are curated to contain meaningful clues to make identifying them possible and gripping.

Where and when was this taken? (Public Domain)

I've spent hours playing Timeguessr not just by myself, but with friends and family. The “detective work” feels rewarding and is open-ended enough that you don't generally have to pick up on any one specific clue to score well in a round, making the game reasonably accessible. Moreover, this means that when playing with others it's likely everyone will have something to contribute. In addition to daily games there is an infinite mode backed by their large album of images, allowing you to play many, many rounds before seeing the same images multiple times becomes a problem. The game looks good and runs well in both their app and in a desktop browser where I'm partial to playing.

Familiar faces overlook a familiar landmark is this round (Wikimedia Commons)

Timeguessr has unique, satisfying gameplay that sets it comfortably ahead of its competition. It's fun to play alone. It's fun to play with others. 5/5 - Near Perfect
