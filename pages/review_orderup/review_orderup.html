Order Up Review

Published: 7/21/2025

Read Length: 5-10 min

NOTE: Order Up is currently not available. The website (orderup.games) states: "Order Up is now paused. Stay tuned for an exciting update about the future of the game!"

Order up is a general-knowledge trivia game where the player orders a handful of list items by a mystery quality alluded to by the theme of that day’s puzzle. Order up is made by Adam Wagner, who not only produces another daily game, the spelling-focused “Anigrams”, but has also built crosswords for several publications including The New York Times.

Every game starts with a theme and seven items that can be placed in order based on it. The player drags the items into order and submits their guess. Items put in their correct spot change color and lock into place and any wrong items can be reordered in subsequent guesses until all of the items are correct or the player exhausts all of their 5 total attempts. A recent puzzle was a list of seven countries and the theme was “Sovereignty”. Not terribly cryptic, the goal of this iteration is to order the countries by the year they attained statehood. Had the theme been “Land” or “Population” the order would have been different. While this particular puzzle’s theme was a bit lacking, here is a place where the creator’s history in crossword building can shine. For example, another recent puzzle called on the player to order “cat” related items like “Proverbial lives”, “NFL Teams”, and “Maximum score on an MCAT”. This theme was not only more playful, engaging, and clever, but because it drew on a more varied knowledge base it made the game more accessible.

When the category is good the game is great. It’s fast, it's fun, and it engages without ever being too demanding. When the theme is underwhelming the puzzle feels more generic, but still doesn’t overstay its welcome due to the incredibly brief time investment required. In my experience the themes are interesting about as often as they are not.

Another gripe with the implementation of this game is the lack of an archive for previous puzzles. Having only gotten into the game fairly recently I've missed nearly a year of Order Up. Wagner clearly puts a lot of effort into these puzzles, but all of the previous entries appear to be unavailable. The creator’s patreon does mention an exclusive archive for his Anigrams puzzle, but it is not clear if subscribers can play old Order Ups.

The visual design of the game is not particularly groundbreaking, but it looks fine and runs well on both desktop and mobile. The mechanic of dragging the items into their proper order feels particularly satisfying on a phone.

Order up generally makes good on its promise to deliver a quick, fun puzzle with a clever theme that justifies returning every 24 hours; it should be high on your list. 4/5 Great game missing some common features.
